7. The Two Mahantarammana Vithis

share

Atimahantarammana Vithi arises because of an object striking the Pasada at the second Citta Khana, after one Citta Khana has passed since arising. Due to reasons such as the Ruparammana being small, being large but distant, light being dim etc., sometimes the Ruparammana strikes the Pasada at the third Citta Khana after two Citta Khanas have passed since arising, or at the fourth Citta Khana after three Citta Khanas have passed. Two Mahantarammana Vithis arise taking the Rupa striking the Pasada at the third and fourth Citta Khanas as object.

The first Mahantarammana Vithi is as follows:- When the Ruparammana strikes the Cakkhu Pasada at the third Citta Khana after two Citta Khanas have passed since arising, just as before, after Bhavanga Calana - Bhavangupaccheda - Pancadvaravajjana - Cakkhu Vinnana - Sampaticchana - Santirana - Votthapana Cittas have arisen in order, some Citta among Kamavacara Javanas arises seven times performing the Javana function. After that, unlike in Atimahantarammana Vithi, Tadarammana does not arise, but Bhavanga Cittas arise. This is the first Mahantarammana Vithi.

This Vithi has twelve Vithi Cittas from Pancadvaravajjana to the seventh Javana. When the two Atita Bhavangas that arose before the Ruparammana struck the Pasada, and the two Bhavanga Calana - Bhavangupaccheda are added to the twelve Vithi Cittas, there are sixteen Cittas. Since the Rupa that became the object of the Vithi started at the Uppada Khana of the first Atita Bhavanga, that Rupa has a lifespan of only one Citta Khana after the seventh Javana. Being very close to cessation, the Rupa which has reached the last Citta Khana is very weak. That weak Ruparammana is not capable of being a condition for a Tadarammana. Therefore, a Tadarammana does not arise in this Vithi. There are various opinions of teachers regarding the arising of Tadarammana. They are not presented here as it is troublesome for beginners.

The second Mahantarammana Vithi is as follows:- The Vithi in which Javana arises seven times after the arising of Bhavanga Calana - Bhavangupaccheda - Pancadvaravajjana - Cakkhu Vinnana - Sampaticchana - Santirana - Votthapana Cittas due to a Ruparammana striking the Cakkhu Pasada at the fourth Citta Khana after three Citta Khanas have passed since arising, is the second Mahantarammana Vithi. In this Vithi, the Ruparammana that became the object of the Vithi ceases together with the seventh Javana.

Six Parittarammana Vithis

The six Vithis arising taking as object the Ruparammana striking the Cakkhu Pasada at the fifth Citta Khana after four Citta Khanas have passed since arising, the Ruparammana striking the Pasada at the sixth Citta Khana after five Citta Khanas have passed, the Ruparammana striking the Pasada at the seventh Citta Khana after six Citta Khanas have passed, the Ruparammana striking the Pasada at the eighth Citta Khana after seven Citta Khanas have passed, the Ruparammana striking the Pasada at the ninth Citta Khana after eight Citta Khanas have passed, and the Rupa striking the Pasada at the tenth Citta Khana after nine Citta Khanas have passed, are called Parittarammana Vithis.

The difference in Mahantarammana Vithis is the absence of Tadarammana. The difference in Parittarammana Vithis is the absence of both Javana and Tadarammana.

When a Ruparammana strikes the Cakkhu Pasada at the fifth Citta Khana after four Citta Khanas have passed since arising, Bhavanga Calana - Bhavangupaccheda - Pancadvaravajjana - Cakkhu Vinnana - Sampaticchana - Santirana - Votthapana Cittas arise in order. Counting those Cittas from Bhavanga Calana, the Votthapana Citta is the seventh Citta. Counting with the four Atita Bhavangas, i.e., Bhavangas passed before striking the Pasada added to the beginning, the Votthapana Citta is the eleventh Citta. The Rupa that became the object of this Vithi arose with the first Atita Bhavanga. Therefore, when Votthapana ceases, the lifespan of that Rupa is eleven Citta Khanas. There are only six Citta Khanas left to complete seventeen. The Rupa that became the object of the Vithi ceases in six Citta Khanas after Votthapana.

It is Citta Niyama that Kamavacara Javana arises seven times when there is no special reason for the number of Javana Citta turns to decrease. Therefore, Javana Cittas do not arise on an object where there is no room for Javana Citta to arise seven times. The Ruparammana mentioned above has only a time of six Citta Khanas left ahead. Since the time is insufficient for Javana to arise seven times, a Javana Citta does not arise in that Parittarammana. In Javana places, instead of Javana, Votthapana Citta itself arises once or twice. After that, Bhavanga descent occurs.

The question "Why can't a seventh Javana arise after the Ruparammana has ceased with the sixth Javana Citta?" arises here. The object of Cittas belonging to one Vithi must be similar not only in Dhamma but also in time. The Rupa becoming the object of this Vithi, having spent the lifespan of seventeen Citta Khanas, after ceasing at the seventeenth Citta Khana belonging to the present time, belongs to the past time. If a Javana arises in this Vithi after the Rupa has ceased, since there is no present Rupa to take as object, it has to take the past Rupa as object. Since it is against Citta Niyama, in this Parittarammana where there is no room for Javana Citta to arise seven times taking the present Rupa as object, Javana does not arise at all; there, after Votthapana, Votthapana itself arises once or twice instead of Javana. Before the Rupa ceases, Bhavanga descent occurs and the Vithi ends.
