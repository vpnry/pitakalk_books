1. Summary of Feeling

share

There are three types of feelings: “Sukha Vedana, Dukkha Vedana, Upekkha Vedana”. In another method, there are five types of feelings: “Sukha Vedana, Dukkha Vedana, Somanassa Vedana, Domanassa Vedana, Upekkha Vedana”.

Here, what is called Vedana is the Vedana Cetasika which is one of the fifty-two Cetasikas. It has been analyzed and shown in various ways by the Tathagata in many places. Vedana is the experiencing of the taste of the object. The taste of the object is experienced as sweet. The taste of the object is experienced as bitter. The taste of the object is experienced as neutral. Apart from these three ways, there are no other ways of experiencing the taste of the object. Therefore, Vedana is threefold according to the experiencing of the taste of the object. Here, Vedana is shown in five ways according to the Indriya Deshana. In the Indriya Deshana, bodily pleasant feeling is called Sukhindriya, mental pleasant feeling is called Somanassindriya, bodily painful feeling is called Dukkhindriya, mental painful feeling is called Domanassindriya, and neutral feeling is called Upekkhindriya.

The Cittas associated with each feeling can be easily found as shown in the Citta Chapter. There is only one Citta with Sukha Sahagata: Ahetuka Kusala Vipaka Kaya Vinnana. There is only one Citta with Dukkha Sahagata: Ahetuka Akusala Vipaka Kaya Vinnana.

The sixty-two Cittas: four Lobha Mula Somanassa Sahagata Cittas, Ahetuka Kusala Vipaka Somanassa Sahagata Santirana, Hasituppada, twelve Kamavacara Sobhana Somanassa Sahagata Cittas, eleven First Jhana Cittas, eleven Second Jhana Cittas, eleven Third Jhana Cittas, and eleven Fourth Jhana Cittas are Somanassa Sahagata Cittas.

The two Dvesha Mula Cittas are Domanassa Sahagata Cittas.

There are fifty-five Upekkha Sahagata Cittas: six in Akusala, fourteen in Ahetuka, twelve in Kama Sobhana, three in Rupavacara, twelve in Arupavacara, and eight in Lokuttara.
