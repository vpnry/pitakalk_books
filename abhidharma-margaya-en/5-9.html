9. Manodvara Vithis

share

Manodvara Vithis are of two parts: Kama Javana Vithi and Appana Javana Vithi. 'Appana Javana' refers to Mahaggata Lokuttara Javanas. There are five Vibhutarammana Vithis arising taking as object a present Rupa having a lifespan of seventeen Citta Khanas. There are two Avibhutarammana Vithis arising taking them as object. In the twenty-eight Rupas, there are twenty-two Rupas having a lifespan of seventeen Citta Khanas each. The other six Rupas do not have that much lifespan. About Rupas, it will be possible to know in the Rupa chapter. There is one Vibhutarammana Vithi and one Avibhutarammana Vithi arising taking as object the remaining Nama Rupa Dharmas which do not have a lifespan of seventeen Citta Khanas, and the twenty-two Rupas belonging to past and future having a lifespan of seventeen Citta Khanas. Thus, there are nine Kamavacara Javana Manodvara Vithis.

Vibhutarammana Vithis having seventeen Citta Khanas

1st Vibhutarammana Vithi.

The Vithi in which some Kamavacara Javana arises seven times and Tadarammana twice after the arising and ceasing of Bhavanga Calana, Bhavangupaccheda, Manodvaravajjana due to a Rupa having a lifespan of seventeen Citta Khanas meeting the Manodvara at the second Citta Khana after one Citta Khana has passed since arising, is the first Saptarasayushka (seventeen-lifespan) Vibhutarammana Vithi. The Rupa which is the object of the Vithi ceases with the fourth Bhavanga.

In these Vithi diagrams, Atita Bhavangas are shown to show the number of Citta Khanas passed before the Ruparammana struck the Manodvara. Bhavangas after Tadarammana are shown to show the time the Rupa which is the object of Vithi Cittas exists.

Two Avibhutarammana Vithis.

Objects striking the Manodvara after six or seven Citta Khanas have passed since arising are weak. They are not clear. Therefore, it is called "Avibhutarammana". In Avibhutarammana Vithi, Tadarammana does not arise. At the end of Javana, Bhavanga descent occurs.

Vibhutarammana Vithi not having seventeen Citta Khanas.

The Vithi in which some Kama Javana arises seven times and Tadarammana twice after Bhavanga Calana, Bhavangupaccheda, Manodvaravajjana due to the meeting of any of the following with the Manodvara: present, past, and future Kamavacara Citta Cetasikas which do not have a lifespan of seventeen Citta Khanas, the two Vinnatti Rupas, the four Lakkhana Rupas, and the twenty-two Saptarasayushka Rupas belonging to past and future; followed by Bhavanga, is the Vibhutarammana Vithi not having seventeen Citta Khanas.

Avibhutarammana Vithi not having seventeen Citta Khanas.

The Vithi in which Tadarammana does not arise but Bhavanga occurs after the arising of Bhavanga Calana, Bhavangupaccheda, Manodvaravajjana and Kama Javana seven times due to the meeting of any of the following with the Manodvara: past, future, and present eighty-nine Cittas, fifty-two Cetasikas, two Vinnapti Rupas, four Lakkhana Rupas, twenty-two past and future Rupas not having a lifespan of seventeen Citta Khanas, timeless Nirvana, and two Pannattis; is the Avibhutarammana Vithi not having seventeen Citta Khanas.

Of these two Vithis, the objects for Vibhutarammana Vithi are few because of Tadarammana. Tadarammana is a weak Citta. It is shown in Arammana Sangaha that it is a Citta taking only Kamavacara Dharmas as object. What Kamavacara Dharmas are is also shown there itself. For a Vithi where Tadarammana arises, nothing other than Kamavacara Dharmas can be the object.

Some Kamavacara Javanas are capable of taking all as object. Therefore, for Avibhutarammana Vithi which is not Tadarammana, objects are many.
