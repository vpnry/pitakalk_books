9. Results of Kamavacara Kusala

share

Patisandhi in Kama Sugati is produced by Kamavacara Kusala. During the Pavatti Kala, all sixteen Cittas: the eight Maha Vipakas and the eight Ahetuka Kusala Vipakas are produced in both Kama and Rupa worlds as appropriate.

Kamavacara Kusala is twofold as Tihetuka Kusala and Dvihetuka Kusala. The four Nana Samprayukta Cittas are Tihetuka. The four Nana Viprayukta Cittas are Dvihetuka. Of them, Tihetuka Kusala is powerful. Dvihetuka Kusala is weak. Tihetuka Kusala is also twofold as Utkrsta (Superior) and Omaka (Inferior). Dvihetuka Kusala is also twofold in the same way.

If one does a Kusala with the expectation of appearing as a superior person among people or with the intention of humiliating others, that Kusala becomes inferior because the intention is impure. If Akusala Cittas arise close to the time of doing the Kusala and after doing the Kusala, the Kusala becomes inferior due to the Akusalas arisen on both sides. If one becomes dissatisfied with the Kusala done, thinking "I did a foolish thing" after doing the Kusala, by that also the Kusala becomes inferior. Omaka Kusala refers to inferior Kusala. The Kusala that is not defiled by self-exaltation or disparaging others, or by defilements arising before and after, and which is rejoiced repeatedly even after doing it thinking "I have done a good thing" is Utkrsta Kusala.

Tihetuka Utkrsta Kusala gives Tihetuka Patisandhi and produces sixteen Vipakas during Pavatti Kala: eight Maha Vipakas and eight Ahetuka Kusala Vipakas. By each of the four Tihetuka Utkrsta Kusala Cittas, Patisandhi in Kama Sugati is given in this way and sixteen Vipakas are produced during Pavatti Kala. Producing these sixteen Vipaka Cittas is not done all at once, but is done on various occasions as pleasant forms, sounds etc. are met.

The Somanassa Sahagata or Upekkha Sahagata nature and Asankharika or Sasankharika nature of a Kusala are not factors that influence the giving of results. Therefore, it should be noted that Upekkha Sahagata Vipaka can arise from Somanassa Sahagata Kusala, Somanassa Sahagata Vipaka from Upekkha Sahagata Kusala, Sasankharika Vipaka from Asankharika Kusala, and Asankharika Vipaka from Sasankharika Kusala.

Tihetuka Omaka Kusala and Dvihetuka Utkrsta Kusala give Dvihetuka Patisandhi, and produce twelve Vipakas during Pavatti Kala: four Nana Vippayutta Maha Vipakas and eight Ahetuka Kusala Vipakas.

Dvihetuka Omaka Kusala gives Ahetuka Patisandhi and produces only eight Ahetuka Kusala Vipakas during Pavatti Kala.

There is also a teacher's view that "Sasankharika Vipaka does not arise from Asankharika Kusala and Asankharika Vipaka does not arise from Sasankharika Kusala". It is not accepted by many teachers. The Asankharika or Sasankharika nature of Cittas is not due to past Kamma, but happens according to the conditions present at the time of the arising of those Cittas.

Results of Rupavacara Kusala

Rupavacara Kusala is also threefold as Paritta (Minor), Madhyama (Medium), and Pranita (Superior). Rupavacara Kusala associated with weak Chanda etc. is Paritta. Kusala associated with medium Chanda etc. is Madhyama. Kusala associated with superior Chanda etc. is Pranita Kusala.

Kamavacara Kusala is a weak Citta. Therefore, many Vipaka Cittas similar and dissimilar to it are produced by it in various places. Rupavacara Kusala is a powerful Citta. Therefore, low Vipaka Cittas dissimilar to it are not produced by it. Results are produced by it only in the Brahma state which is a high place. Even in the Brahma state, only Jhana Cittas similar to the Kusala are produced by it as Patisandhi, Bhavanga, and Cuti, and no other Citta is produced.

By Paritta Rupavacara First Jhana Kusala, they are born in Brahmaparisajja. By Madhyama First Jhana Kusala, they are born in Brahmapurohita. By Pranita First Jhana, they are born in Maha Brahma.

By Paritta Second and Third Jhana Kusalas, they are born in Parittabha. By Madhyama Kusalas, in Appamanabha. By Pranita Kusalas, in Abhassara.

By Paritta Fourth Jhana, they are born in Parittasubha. By Madhyama, in Appamanasubha. By Pranita, in Subhakinha.

By Fifth Jhana, they are born in Vehapphala. By developing the Fifth Jhana itself as Sanna Viraga (dispassion towards perception), they are born among Asanna beings. Some sectarian Kamma proponents, seeing the fault in the mind thinking "When there is a mind, there are attachments, conflicts, and delusions; when there is no mind, they are not there", wish for a mindless existence, develop the Fifth Jhana by Vayo Kasina meditation and are born in Asanna Bhava. They are born there in the posture they died.

Anagamis who have attained Fifth Jhana are born in Suddhavasa. Others are not born in Suddhavasa. Of the Anagamis, those with excessive Saddha are born in Aviha, those with excessive Virya in Atappa, those with excessive Smrti in Sudassa, those with excessive Samadhi in Sudassi, and those with excessive Panna in Akanittha.

Arupa Jhana attainers are born in Akasanancayatanadi Bhumis in order.
