Twelve Ayatanas

share

9th Lesson

Cakkhayatana, Sotayatana, Ghanayatana, Jivhayatana, Kayayatana, Manayatana, Rupayatana, Saddayatana, Gandhayatana, Rasayatana, Photthabbayatana, Dhammayatana are the twelve Ayatanas.

Cakkhayatana is the Cakkhu Prasada. Sota - Ghana - Jivha - Kayayatanas are also the respective Prasada Rupas. Manayatana is the eighty-nine Cittas. Rupayatana is the Vanna Rupa. Saddayatana is the Sabda Rupa. Gandhayatana is the Gandha Rupa. Rasayatana is the Rasa Rupa. Photthabbayatana is the three Bhuta Rupas Pathavi, Tejo, Vayo. Dhammayatana is the fifty-two Cetasikas, sixteen Sukshma Rupas, and Nibbana.

Of these twelve Ayatanas, the six starting with Cakkhayatana are called Ajjhattikayatanas. The six starting with Rupayatana are called Bahirayatanas.

What are shown by the name Ayatana are the same Paramartha Dhammas shown by the name Skandha. The only difference is that Nibbana is also included in the Ayatana classification. The group of Paramartha Dhammas is divided into twelve by the name Ayatana according to the functioning of Citta and Cetasikas. In Cakkhayatana and Rupayatana, Cakkhudvarika Citta and Cetasikas function. In Sotayatana and Saddayatana, Sotadvarika Citta and Cetasikas function. In Ghanayatana and Gandhayatana, Ghanadvarika Citta and Cetasikas function. In Jivhayatana and Rasayatana, Jivhadvarika Citta and Cetasikas function. In Kayayatana and Photthabbayatana, Kayadvarika Citta and Cetasikas function. In Manayatana and Dhammayatana, Manodvarika Citta and Cetasikas function. Cakkhu Prasada etc. are called Ayatana in the sense that they are the places where Citta and Cetasikas function. The places where Citta and Cetasikas arise and perform their respective functions are called Ayatanas.

Some people firmly take Nama Dhammas which perform actions such as knowing, feeling, perceiving, enjoying, opposing etc. as soul. Their Sakkaya Ditthi is not dispelled by doing Vipassana by way of Skandha method. Their Sakkaya Ditthi is dispelled by doing Vipassana by way of Ayatana method. Therefore, for them, the group of Paramartha Dhammas is divided into twelve parts as Ayatanas.
