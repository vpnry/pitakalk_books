14. Aryan Persons

share

Lesson 17

Persons who have practiced Vipassana meditation and generated Supramundane Path and Fruit are Aryan persons. Others are worldlings (Puthujjana).

There are eight Aryan persons: Sotapatti Maggattha person, Sotapatti Phalattha person, Sakadagami Maggattha person, Sakadagami Phalattha person, Anagami Maggattha person, Anagami Phalattha person, Arahatta Maggattha person, and Arahatta Phalattha person. There are four Maggattha persons. There are four Phalattha persons. A person exists as a Maggattha person only at the moment when the Supramundane Path Consciousness exists in his continuity. That is a very short time. Therefore, a Maggattha person cannot be found. With the cessation of the Path Consciousness, the Fruit Consciousness arises, so the Maggattha person becomes a Phalattha person. Since the Maggattha person stage is momentary, the names Sotapanna person, Sakadagami person, Anagami person, and Arahant person are mostly used for Phalattha persons.

Abandoning of Defilements

The two defilements, Ditthi (View) and Vicikiccha (Doubt), are completely abandoned by the Sotapatti Magga. Other defilements that are gross enough to cause rebirth in Apaya are also abandoned.

In terms of consciousness, it is as follows:- The four Ditthi-sampayutta consciousnesses and the Vicikiccha-sahagata consciousness, these five consciousnesses are abandoned without remainder by the Sotapatti Magga. The four Ditthi-vippayutta consciousnesses and the two Dosa-mula consciousnesses that arise grossly enough to cause birth in Apaya are also abandoned.

The four Ditthi-vippayutta consciousnesses, the two Dosa-mula consciousnesses, and the Uddhacca-sahagata consciousness which are not gross enough to cause birth in Apaya remain for the Sotapanna person.

Gross Lobha, Dosa, and Moha are abandoned by the Sakadagami Magga. Those defilements arise rarely for the Sakadagami person. Even when they arise rarely, they do not arise in a gross manner.

In terms of consciousness, it is as follows:- The gross four Ditthi-vippayutta consciousnesses and the two Dosa-mula consciousnesses are abandoned by the Sakadagami Magga. The subtle four Ditthi-vippayutta consciousnesses, the two Dosa-mula consciousnesses, and the Uddhacca-sahagata consciousness remain for the Sakadagami person.

Kamaraga and Vyapada are completely abandoned by the Anagami Magga. In terms of consciousness, it is as follows:- The four Ditthi-vippayutta consciousnesses with Kamaraga and the two Dosa-mula consciousnesses are abandoned without remainder by the Anagami Magga.

The four Ditthi-vippayutta consciousnesses with Ruparaga - Aruparaga and the Uddhacca-sahagata consciousness remain for the Anagami person.

Ruparaga, Aruparaga, Mana, Uddhacca, and Avijja are abandoned without remainder by the Arahatta Magga.

In terms of consciousness, it is as follows:- The four subtle Ditthi-vippayutta consciousnesses with Ruparaga - Aruparaga - Mana and the Uddhacca-sahagata consciousness are abandoned by the Arahatta Magga. There are no remaining defilements for the Arahant person.

Attainments (Samapatti)

There are three types of attainments: "Jhana Samapatti, Phala Samapatti, Nirodha Samapatti".

Dwelling with a certain Rupavacara or Arupavacara Jhana consciousness is called "Jhana Samapatti". For the person meditating for that purpose at the stage of attaining Jhana for the first time, after Kamavacara Javana consciousnesses arise and cease three or four times under the names Parikamma - Upacara - Anuloma - Gotrabhu, the Jhana consciousness arises once and becomes Bhavanga. The person who generates Jhana for the first time is called "Adikammika". If the person who has thus attained Jhana once meditates again to generate that Jhana consciousness, after Kamavacara Javana arises and ceases three or four times as before, the Jhana consciousness arises for many turns. According to the development of the yogi's concentration, the Jhana consciousness itself arises continuously for thousands and hundreds of thousands of times without becoming Bhavanga. He can dwell like that even for hours. That is Jhana Samapatti.

Dwelling with Supramundane Fruit Consciousness is Phala Samapatti. If a person who has attained a Supramundane Fruit once meditates again to generate it, for him too, Phala consciousnesses arise continuously for thousands and hundreds of thousands of times without becoming Bhavanga, just like Jhana consciousnesses.

Dwelling having completely stopped the arising of consciousness is Nirodha Samapatti. Only Anagamis and Arahants who have attained the eight attainments can enter Nirodha Samapatti. They enter Nirodha Samapatti wishing to experience the bliss of Nirvana in the present life itself. Anagamis and Arahants in the Arupa existence also cannot do it.

When an Anagami or Arahant person with eight attainments wishes to enter Nirodha Samapatti, sitting in a secluded place, he first enters the first Jhana, rises from it, and contemplates the formations belonging to that first Jhana as impermanent, suffering, and non-self. Then he enters the second Jhana, rises from it, and contemplates the formations belonging to it as impermanent etc. Then he enters the third Jhana, rises from it, and contemplates the formations belonging to it as impermanent etc. Thus doing up to Akincannayatana Jhana, entering Nevasannanasannayatana Jhana, rising from it, performing the four preliminary duties: Nanabaddha-avikopana, Sangha-patimanana, Satthu-pakkosana, Addhana-pariccheda, he enters Nevasannanasannayatana Jhana. After the Nevasannanasannayatana consciousness arises once or twice, the arising of consciousness in that person stops. That is Nirodha Samapatti. After the time determined to dwell in Nirodha has passed, if the person who entered it is an Anagami person, the Anagami Phala consciousness arises once, and if an Arahant person, the Arahatta Phala consciousness arises once, and then falls into Bhavanga. After that, consciousnesses begin to arise as usual.

Of the four preliminary duties, Nanabaddha-avikopana is determining "If there is any requisite not connected to my body, may they not be destroyed during the time I am in Nirodha Samapatti". No danger from fire, water etc. happens to the body of the person who has entered Nirodha Samapatti or to an object connected to the body during the Nirodha Samapatti time. That is the power of the attainment itself. Since requisites not connected to the body are not protected so, one should make a determination regarding them.

Sangha-patimanana is determining "If the Sangha needs me for a Vinaya act, may I rise from the attainment at that moment!". Satthu-pakkosana is determining "If the Buddha wants to meet me, may I rise from the attainment at that very moment!".

Addhana-pariccheda is "considering one's life span". If death is to happen during the Nirodha Samapatti time, one has to rise from the attainment. Therefore, one should look at the time life lasts.
