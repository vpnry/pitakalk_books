5. Analysis of Matter

share

All Rupas are Ahetuka because they are devoid of roots like Lobha etc. They are called Sappaccaya because they arise from causes like Kamma etc. They are called Sasava because they are objects for Asavas. They are called Sankhata because they are produced by causes like Kamma etc. They are called Laukika because they belong to the world. They are called Kamavacara because they are objects for sensual craving. They are called Anarammana because they do not take objects. They are called Appahatabba because they are not to be abandoned like defilements.

The five Prasada Rupas are called Ajjhattika Rupas. The remaining twenty-three Rupas are called Bahira Rupas.

Generally, all Rupas belonging to the body of a being are called Ajjhattika Rupas because they are regarded as self, saying 'mine', 'I'. Among the group of Rupas belonging to the self-state, the five Prasada Rupas are specially given the name Ajjhattika because the regard as self and craving towards them exist excessively.

The six Rupas namely five Prasada Rupas and Hadaya Rupa are called Vatthu Rupas. The remaining twenty-two Rupas are called Avatthu Rupas. The name Vatthu Rupa is given to six Rupas because they are bases for consciousnesses.

The seven Rupas namely five Prasada Rupas and two Vinnatti Rupas are called Dvara Rupas. The remaining twenty-one Rupas are called Advara Rupas. The five Prasada Rupas are doors for the five consciousnesses. The two Vinnatti Rupas are doors for bodily and verbal actions.

The eight Rupas namely five Prasada Rupas, two Bhava Rupas, and Jivita Rupa are called Indriya Rupas. The remaining twenty Rupas are called Anindriya Rupas. Indriya refers to Dhammas that exercise lordship over something. The five Prasada Rupas exercise lordship over the five consciousnesses. The five consciousnesses arise in conformity with the Prasadas. The two Bhava Rupas exercise lordship over other Rupas by imparting female and male natures.

The twelve Rupas namely five Prasada Rupas and seven Gocara Rupas are Olarika Rupas. Also Santike Rupas. Also Sappatigha Rupas. The sixteen Rupas namely Apo Dhatu, Stri Bhava, Purusa Bhava, Hadaya Rupa, Jivita Rupa, Ahara Rupa, Pariccheda Rupa, five Vikara Rupas, and four Laksana Rupas are Sukhuma Rupas. Also Dure Rupas. Also Appatigha Rupas.

It was said 'seven Gocara Rupas' because Sprastavya consists of three Rupas: Pathavi, Tejo, Vayo.

Prasada Rupas and Visaya Rupas are Olarika because they strike each other. They are called Olarika because they are not subtle. The meaning of the word Santike Rupa is 'Rupas that are near'. Since Prasada Rupas and Gocara Rupas are gross, they can be easily known. Therefore, even if they are far, they are like being near. That is why they are given the name Santike Rupa. They are given the name Sappatigha Rupa because they strike each other.

The eighteen Rupas produced by Kamma are called Upadinna Rupas. The remaining ten Rupas are called Anupadinna. Which Rupas are produced by Kamma will be known later. The meaning of the word Upadinna is 'that which is grasped by craving and views as mine and I'. All bodily Rupas are grasped by craving and views as mine and I. Since Kammaja Rupas are the main part of the Rupa body, that grasping is stronger in Kammaja Rupas. Therefore, they are specially given the name Upadinna.

The first of the Gocara Rupas, Varna Rupa, is called Sanidassana Rupa. Its meaning is 'visible Rupa'. Of the twenty-eight Rupas, only Varna Rupa is seen by the eye. The other twenty-seven Rupas are called Anidassana Rupas because they are not seen.

The five Prasada Rupas are called Gocaraggahika Rupas because they take objects like Rupa etc. The remaining twenty-three Rupas are called Agocaraggahika because they do not take objects.

Among Prasada Rupas, Cakkhu and Sota are called Asampattaggahaka because they take only objects that are far. Only a thing that is far and strikes the eye is seen by the eye. If something strikes and stays in the eye itself, it is not seen by the eye. The eyelid is not seen because it stays striking the eye. Sound heard by the ear is also the sound that is far. That is why the listener feels the direction and place of the sound. Ghana-Jivha-Kaya Prasadas are not capable of taking objects situated far.

The eight Rupas namely four: Varna-Gandha-Rasa-Oja, and four Bhuta Rupas are called Avinibbhoga Rupas because they are not inseparable from each other. The remaining twenty Rupas are called Vinibbhoga Rupas.
