Upekkha Meditation

share

Upekkha is the neutrality maintained towards beings. Only the fifth Jhana can be obtained from Upekkha meditation. The fifth Jhana can be obtained by developing Upekkha only by yogis who have already obtained the first, second, third, and fourth Jhanas from another meditation.

Those who wish to develop Upekkha should consider the nature of the world with wisdom and be neutral towards beings. The eight worldly conditions such as gain and loss come to all beings. The nature of the world does not change according to one's wish. "Everyone is subject to worldly conditions. There is no one capable of maintaining the world according to one's wish. These beings receive happiness and suffering according to their own kamma. Beings come to suffering by going on the wrong path. They come to happiness by going on the right path. They do not become happy by someone wishing them to be happy. They do not become free from suffering by wishing them to be free from suffering. Therefore, worrying about the world is a useless effort." Thus, considering the nature of the world, one should practice Upekkha thinking "These beings receive happiness and suffering according to their own kamma".
