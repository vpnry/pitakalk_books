Ogha and Yoga

share

There are four Oghas namely Kamogha - Bhavogha - Ditthogha - Avijjogha.

There are four Yogas namely Kama Yoga - Bhava Yoga - Ditthi Yoga - Avijja Yoga.

Kamogha - Kama Yoga etc. can be understood according to Asavas. The great mass of water flowing overwhelming fields - estates - villages is called 'Ogha'. Drowning the being caught in it, not letting him escape easily is the nature of Ogha. The three Lobhadi Dhammas are called "Ogha" because they are like a mass of water as they drown beings in Samsara like a flowing mass of water, and because they do not let him escape as long as those Dhammas exist in the continuity of the being. The Dhammas that drown beings in the ocean of Samsara and do not let them cross the ocean of Samsara are Oghas. Those same Dhammas are called Yoga because they yoke beings again and again in the wheel of Samsara. The same Dhamma is called "Asava" in the sense of intoxicating etc., "Ogha" because of drowning beings in the ocean of Samsara, and "Yoga" because of yoking in the wheel of Samsara.
