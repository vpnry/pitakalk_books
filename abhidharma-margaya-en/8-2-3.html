Magganga

share

There are twelve Maggangas namely Samma Ditthi - Samma Sankappa - Samma Vaca - Samma Kammanta - Samma Ajiva - Samma Vayama - Samma Sati - Samma Samadhi - Miccha Ditthi - Miccha Sankappa - Miccha Vayama - Miccha Samadhi.

Here Samma Ditthi is the Panna Cetasika. Samma Sankappa is the Vitakka Cetasika. Samma Vaca etc. three are the Virati Cetasikas. Samma Vayama is the Viriya Cetasika. Samma Sati is the Sati Cetasika. Samma Samadhi is the Ekaggata Cetasika. Miccha Ditthi is the Akusala Ditthi Cetasika. Miccha Sankappa is the Vitakka Cetasika associated with Akusala Cittas. Miccha Vayama is the Viriya Cetasika associated with Akusala Cittas. Miccha Samadhi is the Ekaggata Cetasika in Akusala Cittas except the Vicikiccha-sahagata Citta. It should be remembered that Ekaggata in Vicikiccha-sahagata Citta does not reach the state of Indriya or Bala either.

Since the three Cetasikas Vitakka - Viriya - Ekaggata are applied in two places each, there are nine Maggangas by way of Dhammas. Panna - Vitakka - Samma Vaca - Samma Kammanta - Samma Ajiva - Viriya - Sati - Ekaggata - Ditthi are the nine Cetasikas which are Maggangas.

These Dhammas Samma Ditthi etc., becoming groups, direct the mind towards unhappy states in the Akusala domain, and towards happy states and Nibbana in the Kusala Kriya domain. Samma Ditthi etc. are called Maggangas because they are factors of the paths leading to unhappy states, happy states, and Nibbana. Here, Magga means the means.

In the Pranaghata Kamma, the four-factored Jhana namely Vitakka - Vicara - Domanassa - Ekaggata is found, and the path consisting of three factors namely Miccha Sankappa - Miccha Vayama - Miccha Samadhi is found. Thus, how Jhana and Magga are obtained in all Kusala Akusala domains should be understood according to the Cetasikas associated with those respective Cittas.
