Ahara

share

There are four Aharas namely Kabalinkara Ahara, Phassa Ahara, Manosancetana Ahara, Vinnana Ahara.

The meaning of the word Ahara is 'the cause that produces a certain fruit'. There are many causes that produce various things. Among those causes, the four starting with Kabalinkara Ahara are given the name 'Ahara' because they are special causes for the arising and existence of Nama Rupas belonging to the internal continuum.

Kabalinkara Ahara is the nutritive essence (Oja) of the food consumed. By it, Aharaja Rupas are produced in the body. The power of a paddy seed is primary for producing a paddy plant. Fertilizer and water are the main causes for the growth and existence of the produced paddy plant for months. Similarly, for beings living on Kabalinkara Ahara, Kamma is primary for producing Rupas at Patisandhi. Thereafter, Utu and Ahara are the two main causes for the growth and living for a long time of the being. The lineage of Kammaja Rupas exists until death by the support of Utu and Ahara. Utu also can perform its function only if it receives the help of Ahara. Therefore, the support of Ahara is primary for the growth and living for a long time of the being. Therefore, Kabalinkara Ahara has been preached as a special cause.

Phassa Ahara is the Phassa Cetasika. By it, Vedana is produced. Birds live by taking food with their beaks. If the beak is lost, they will die without a way to take food. Similarly, these beings, obtaining the taste of the object due to Phassa, generating Vedanas, generate Tanha and prolong Samsara. If there is no Phassa, Vedana and Tanha cannot arise and Samsara will be cut off. Phassa is a main cause for the continuity of Samsara. Therefore, it has been preached by the name Ahara.

Manosancetana Ahara is the Kusala Akusala Cetana. By it, Patisandhi is produced in the three existences Kama - Rupa - Arupa. Birds live by flying from tree to tree and finding food. Similarly, these beings continue Samsara going from existence to existence by the power of Kamma. If there is no Kamma sending the being again and again from existence to existence, the continuity of Samsara will not occur. Kamma is also a special cause for the existence of Samsara continuously without interruption. Therefore, the name Ahara has been given to it too.

Vinnana Ahara is the Citta. By it, at Patisandhi, Nama Dhammas such as Phassa, Vedana etc. and Kammaja Rupas are produced. During the course of existence (Pavatti), Cittaja Rupas and Cetasika Dhammas are produced by it. If there is no Vinnana, Nama Rupas will not arise and Samsara will break. Vinnana is also a main cause for the continuity of Samsara. Therefore, the name Ahara has been given to it too.

Of the four Aharas, Kabalinkara Ahara is a Rupa Ahara. The remaining three are Arupas.
