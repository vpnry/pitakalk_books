Patipada Nanadassana Visuddhi

share

There are ten Vipassana knowledges: Sammasana Nana, Udayabbaya Nana, Bhanganupassana Nana, Bhayatupatthana Nana, Adinavanupassana Nana, Nibbidanupassana Nana, Muncitukamyata Nana, Patisankhanupassana Nana, Sankharupekkha Nana, and Anuloma Nana.

Sammasana Nana

The knowledge that knows the three characteristics such as impermanence of Nama and Rupa dhammas is called Sammasana Nana. It has already been attained by the yogi who has developed Maggamagga Nanadassana Visuddhi. What he has to do next is to meditate to develop the nine knowledges starting from Udayabbaya Nana. The nine Vipassana knowledges starting from Udayabbaya Nana up to Anuloma Nana that arise in the future for the person who has developed Maggamagga Nanadassana Visuddhi while doing Vipassana is Patipada Nanadassana Visuddhi.

Udayabbaya Nana

The Vipassana knowledge that sees how new formations arise that did not exist before and how all arisen formations break up is called 'Udayabbaya Nana'.

Bhanganupassana Nana

As the knowledge of the yogi who practices Vipassana by looking at the arising and breaking up of formations that are caught by his mind develops and sharpens, very quickly many formations appear to his mind, and he does not have time to recall the arising and breaking up of all of them. Then the yogi gives up attending to the arising of the formations that appear and starts looking only at their dissolution. Just as bubbles of water are seen breaking up when looking at a reservoir when it is raining, the breaking up of formations in his own continuity begins to appear to his wisdom. That knowledge which sees the breaking up of formations is called 'Bhanganupassana Nana'.

Bhayatupatthana Nana

To the yogi who sees formations breaking up everywhere in the three existences of Kama, Rupa, and Arupa like mustard seeds popping in a pan, the group of breaking up formations begins to appear as something to be feared. That Vipassana knowledge which sees formations as fearful is called 'Bhayatupatthana Nana'.

Adinavanupassana Nana

The breaking up of formations cannot be stopped by anyone by any means. There is no refuge for them. They are helpless. The Vipassana knowledge that sees that helplessness of formations is 'Adinavanupassana Nana'.

Nibbidanupassana Nana

By seeing the helplessness of the group of formations which the yogi had clung to as 'I' and 'mine' in the past, disenchantment arises in the yogi regarding them. The yogi becomes weary of the multitude of formations. The Vipassana knowledge that has the nature of being disenchanted with formations is called 'Nibbidanupassana Nana'.

Muncitukamyata Nana

The Vipassana knowledge accompanied by the desire to be released from those disenchanted formations is called 'Muncitukamyata Nana'.

Patisankhanupassana Nana

The extensive Vipassana knowledge of the yogi who wishes to be released from formations, contemplating formations in detail in various ways as a means to be released from them, is called "Patisankhanupassana Nana".

Sankharupekkha Nana

Having seen the great mass of faults of formations with Patisankhanupassana Nana, and now happy that 'I have found the way to be released from suffering', the Vipassana knowledge that proceeds with even effort, abandoning the strong effort to examine formations, is 'Sankharupekkha Nana'. Although devoid of the effort of examining formations, this knowledge is stronger than all the previous knowledges. Just as a stone thrown into the sky falls towards the earth very fast without effort, this knowledge also proceeds very fast automatically in the characteristics such as impermanence of formations.

Anuloma Nana

When Sankharupekkha Nana develops and the time is very close for the Supramundane Path Knowledge to arise, the Vipassana knowledge that arises in conformity with the eight Vipassana knowledges such as Udayabbaya Nana and the Supramundane Path Knowledge is called 'Anuloma Nana'.
