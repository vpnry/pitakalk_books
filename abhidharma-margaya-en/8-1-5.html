Nivarana

share

There are six Nivaranas namely Kamacchanda Nivarana, Vyapada Nivarana, Thinamiddha Nivarana, Uddhacca Kukkucca Nivarana, Vicikiccha Nivarana, Avijja Nivarana.

The Dhammas which cover, obstruct Kusala so that unarisen Kusala does not arise and arisen Kusala does not continue in the continuity of beings are called Nivarana.

Strong Lobha is Kamacchanda Nivarana. Dosa is Vyapada Nivarana. Thinamiddha Nivarana etc. are the Cetasikas indicated by those names. Avijja Nivarana is Moha Cetasika.

By way of Cetasika Dhammas, there are eight Nivaranas namely Lobha - Dosa - Thina - Middha - Uddhacca - Kukkucca - Vicikiccha - Moha.

Thina - Middha two Cetasikas and Uddhacca - Kukkucca two Cetasikas are preached as one Nivarana each because of similarities in terms of function, cause of arising, and opposing Dhammas. Both Thina - Middha Cetasikas perform the single function of shrinking the mind. Both Uddhacca - Kukkucca Cetasikas perform the function of agitating the mind. The cause for both Thina - Middha is laziness and inactivity of the body. The cause for both Uddhacca - Kukkucca is thinking about loss of relatives etc. Both Thina - Middha are opposed to Viriya. Both Uddhacca - Kukkucca are opposed to Samatha. Because of these similarities, Thina - Middha two are preached as one Nivarana, and Uddhacca - Kukkucca two are preached as one Nivarana.
