10. The Eighteen Ahetuka Cittas

share

In this Dhamma, the six mental factors (Cetasikas) namely Lobha (Greed), Dosa (Hatred), Moha (Delusion), Alobha (Non-greed), Adosa (Non-hatred), and Amoha (Non-delusion) are called Hetu (Roots). A Citta associated with at least one of these six Cetasikas called Hetu is called a Sahetuka Citta (Rooted Consciousness). Cittas in which not a single Hetu Dhamma is found are called Ahetuka Cittas (Rootless Consciousness). There are three parts of Ahetuka Cittas: Akusala Vipaka (Unwholesome Resultant), Kusala Vipaka (Wholesome Resultant), and Kriya (Functional). It should be specially remembered that the name Ahetuka is used for these Cittas not because they arise automatically without any cause, but because none of the six Dhammas called Hetu (Lobha etc.) are present in them. (A description of Hetu will be given in a future chapter.) Since there are seven Akusala Vipaka Cittas, eight Kusala Vipaka Cittas, and three Kriya Cittas, there are eighteen (18) Ahetuka Cittas.

Vipaka (Resultant) means Cittas that arise in the future in the continuity of the person who performed Kusala (Wholesome) and Akusala (Unwholesome) deeds, due to those Kusala and Akusala deeds. They are Cittas caused to arise in the future by the power of Kusala and Akusala.

The Seven Akusala Vipaka Cittas

Although Akusala Vipaka is generally one, there are seven Akusala Vipaka Cittas based on the places where the Cittas arise and the functions performed by the Cittas.

There are six places where Cittas arise: the Eye, the Ear, the Nose, the Tongue, the Body, and the Heart Base (Hadaya Rupa). (Details about these will be known later.) Among Vipaka Cittas, there are Cittas arising in the eye. There are Cittas arising in the ear, Cittas arising in the nose, Cittas arising in the tongue, and Cittas arising in the body. Names have been given to those Cittas according to the place of arising. Since Cittas arising in the Heart Base are many, they cannot be named according to the place of origin. For Cittas arising in the heart among Ahetuka Cittas, names have been given according to the function performed by each Citta.

  1. Upekkha-sahagata Cakkhu Vinnana (Eye Consciousness associated with Neutral Feeling)
  2. Upekkha-sahagata Sota Vinnana (Ear Consciousness associated with Neutral Feeling)
  3. Upekkha-sahagata Ghana Vinnana (Nose Consciousness associated with Neutral Feeling)
  4. Upekkha-sahagata Jivha Vinnana (Tongue Consciousness associated with Neutral Feeling)
  5. Dukkha-sahagata Kaya Vinnana (Body Consciousness associated with Pain)
  6. Upekkha-sahagata Sampaticchana (Receiving Consciousness associated with Neutral Feeling)
  7. Upekkha-sahagata Santirana (Investigating Consciousness associated with Neutral Feeling)

These seven are Akusala Vipaka Cittas.

The meaning of Cakkhu Vinnana is 'the Citta arising in the eye'. By it, the seeing of the form that has come before the eye is performed.

The meaning of Sota Vinnana is 'the Citta arising in the ear'. By it, the hearing of sounds coming to the ear is performed.

The meaning of Ghana Vinnana is 'the Citta arising in the nose'. By it, the smelling of odors coming to the nose is performed.

The meaning of Kaya Vinnana is 'the Citta arising in the body'. By it, the touches occurring on the body are known.

The Citta that accepts the object taken by the five consciousnesses such as Eye Consciousness is called the Sampaticchana Citta.

The Citta that investigates the object taken by Sampaticchana is called Santirana. (The nature of Sampaticchana and Santirana Cittas can be known in the Vithi section. For now, remember only the number of Cittas.)

The Eight Ahetuka Kusala Vipaka Cittas

  1. Upekkha-sahagata Cakkhu Vinnana
  2. Upekkha-sahagata Sota Vinnana
  3. Upekkha-sahagata Ghana Vinnana
  4. Upekkha-sahagata Jivha Vinnana
  5. Sukha-sahagata Kaya Vinnana (Body Consciousness associated with Pleasure)
  6. Upekkha-sahagata Sampaticchana
  7. Somanassa-sahagata Santirana (Investigating Consciousness associated with Joy)
  8. Upekkha-sahagata Santirana

Thus, there are eight Ahetuka Kusala Vipaka Cittas.

Akusala Cittas produce only Ahetuka Vipaka. Kusala Cittas produce both Ahetuka and Sahetuka Vipaka. What has been stated are the Ahetuka Vipaka Cittas produced by Kusala. The Sahetuka Vipaka produced by Kusala can be known later. It should also be remembered that there are only Ahetuka Kusala Vipaka Cittas, and there is no group of Ahetuka Kusala Cittas.

The difference in Ahetuka Kusala Vipaka compared to Akusala Vipaka is that the Kaya Vinnana is Sukha-sahagata (associated with pleasure), and there are two Santiranas.

There are two Santiranas according to the objects. When the object is very good, the Santirana is Somanassa-sahagata. When the object is medium, it is Upekkha-sahagata.
