12. Maranasanna Vithis

share

Maranasanna Vithis are sixfold as Cakkhudvara Vithi - Sotadvara Vithi - Ghanadvara Vithi - Jihvadvara Vithi - Kayadvara Vithi - Manodvara Vithi. Of them, Cakkhudvara Maranasanna Vithi is fourfold as: Vithi where Cuti occurs after Javana, Vithi where Cuti occurs after Javana-Bhavanga, Vithi where Cuti occurs after Tadarammana, and Vithi where Cuti occurs after Tadarammana-Bhavanga. Of the things that appear at the moment of death, Kamma appears only to the Mind Door. Sometimes, a thing present before the eye at the moment of death can itself be the Kamma Nimitta. Cakkhudvara Maranasanna Vithi arises in such an occasion. Rarely, Gati Nimitta can also be seen by the eye. In such occasions, Cakkhudvara Maranasanna Vithis taking Gati Nimitta as object can also arise.

The Cakkhudvara Maranasanna Vithi where Cuti occurs after Javana is as follows:-

If a Ruparammana which is a Kamma Nimitta comes to the Eye Door at the moment of death, after the arising and ceasing of Atita Bhavanga - Bhavanga Calana - Bhavangupaccheda - Pancadvaravajjana - Cakkhu Vinnana - Sampaticchana - Santirana - Votthapana Cittas in order, and after the arising and ceasing of some Kamavacara Javana five times in order, the Cuti Citta which is the last Citta of the present life arises and ceases. Immediately after that, some Kamavacara Patisandhi Citta arises and ceases. Immediately after that, in the new life, after that very Patisandhi Citta arises and ceases fifteen or sixteen times performing the function of Bhavanga, Manodvaravajjana Citta taking the present life as object arises, and after that a Lobha Mula Javana arises seven times as attachment to the present life. After that, Bhavanga Patana occurs.

In this Maranasanna Vithi, the object for the Cittas from Pancadvaravajjana up to the fifth Javana is the Atita Bhavanga and the arisen present Rupa. From Atita Bhavanga up to the fifth Javana, there are thirteen Cittas. That Rupa has a life of another four Citta moments after the fifth Javana. Therefore, the object for the Patisandhi Citta and the two Bhavangas arising after it is the present Rupa that was the object for Pancadvaravajjana etc. Cittas. That Ruparammana ceases after spending its life of seventeen Citta moments along with the second Bhavanga Citta arising after Patisandhi. Therefore, the object for the Bhavanga Cittas arising after that is the past Ruparammana which was the Kamma Nimitta.

In this Vithi, the object for Atita Bhavanga - Bhavanga Calana - Bhavangupaccheda and Cuti is one of Kamma etc. that was the object for the Maranasanna Javanas of the immediately preceding past life. Since Kammaja Rupas do not arise in the dying person's body after the arising moment of the seventeenth Citta from the Cuti Citta, the support (Nissaya) for Pancadvaravajjana etc. Cittas in this Vithi is the seventeenth Citta from the Cuti Citta and the arisen Hadaya Vatthu. The support for Cakkhu Vinnana is also the seventeenth Citta from the Cuti Citta and the arisen Cakkhu Vatthu. When Patisandhi arises, the Vatthu Rupa supporting that Citta also arises simultaneously with it. The support for the Patisandhi Citta is the Vatthu Rupa arising simultaneously with that Citta. The support for the Bhavanga etc. Cittas arising after that is the Citta born before each respective Citta and the arisen Hadaya Vatthu Rupa. Sota - Ghana - Jivha - Kayadvara Maranasanna Vithis should also be understood according to Cakkhudvara Vithis. The only difference is the application of Srotra Vinnana etc. in places where Cakkhu Vinnana is applied. Sotadvara Maranasanna Vithis can arise for those who die while listening to Dhamma. Ghanadvara Maranasanna Vithis can arise for those who die while smelling the scent of offerings etc. Jihvadvara Maranasanna Vithis can arise for those into whose mouth something is poured at the moment of death, and Kayadvara Maranasanna Vithis can arise for those who die while receiving the touch of someone. Any of the three: Kamma, Kamma Nimitta, Gati Nimitta becomes the object for Kamavacara Patisandhi. Only Kamma Nimitta becomes the object for Rupavacara and Arupavacara Patisandhis. For them, the Kasina Nimitta etc. which are the objects of the Jhanas they have attained become the object as Kamma Nimitta.

For beings born in Asanna Bhava, there is no arising of Patisandhi Citta after Cuti. For them, after Cuti, Jivita Navaka Rupa Kalapas arise as Patisandhi in Asanna Bhava. Therefore, they are Rupa Patisandhikas. Beings born in Arupa Bhumi are Arupa Patisandhikas. Since Rupas also arise when other Patisandhis arise, except Asanna beings and Arupa beings, others are Ruparupa Patisandhikas.

From the four Arupavacara Cutis, except the lower Arupavacara Patisandhis, the remaining Arupavacara Patisandhis and Kama Tihetuka Patisandhis can arise. For one falling from a higher Arupa Bhumi, a lower Arupa Patisandhi does not arise. For those falling from all Arupa Bhumis, Rupavacara Patisandhi or Kamavacara Ahetuka Dvihetuka Patisandhis do not arise. From Rupavacara Cutis, except Ahetuka Patisandhis, remaining Patisandhis can arise. From the Cuti of a Tihetuka person in Kama Loka, all Patisandhis can arise. From Dvihetuka Ahetuka Cutis, only Kamavacara Patisandhis can arise.

For the person falling after living in Asanna Bhumi for five hundred Kalpas, there is no Cuti Citta. The Jivita Navaka Rupa Kalapas arising for the last time are his Cuti. He has no appearance of Kamma - Kamma Nimitta - Gati Nimitta. Taking as object one of the Kamma etc. presented by the Upacara Cetanas of the Fifth Jhana he had attained before coming to Asanna Bhava, Kamavacara Patisandhi arises for him after Asanna Sattva Cuti.

Thus, for beings conceived in existence, after the cessation of the Patisandhi Citta, that very Citta taking that very object arises repeatedly throughout that whole life whenever there is no other Citta. Since the being lives, his Citta lineage is not cut off, because that Citta arises repeatedly. Since it arises as a support for the existence of Bhava, that Citta is called Bhavanga. The last Citta of the Bhava is also that very Citta. When it arises as the last Citta, it is called Cuti Citta.

Thus, just as Patisandhi Bhavanga Cittas and Vithi Cittas and Cuti arise in this present life, in future lives too, by the arising of Patisandhi etc., this Citta lineage continues rolling as one lineage until it reaches Nibbana.

Wise ones, seeing the danger of having to die and be born repeatedly due to the unbroken continuity of the Citta lineage in this way, attain Magga Phala through right practice, cut off the bond of Tanha and go to Nirvana where there is no death and birth.

The Fifth Chapter of Abhidharma Margaya ends here.
