17. The Eight Lokuttara Cittas

share

The eight mental factors (Cetasikas) namely Samma Ditthi (Right View), Samma Sankappa (Right Intention), Samma Vaca (Right Speech), Samma Kammanta (Right Action), Samma Ajiva (Right Livelihood), Samma Vayama (Right Effort), Samma Sati (Right Mindfulness), and Samma Samadhi (Right Concentration) are called the 'Arya Ashtangika Marga' (Noble Eightfold Path). These factors gradually develop in the mind of the meditator who practices Vipassana meditation with the desire to cross the world of Samsara. The Eightfold Path, which has developed to the extent that it can realize Nibbana and eradicate defilements so that they will never arise again in the future, is called "Lokuttara Marga" (Supramundane Path) because it transports the being across the world to Nibbana. The Citta associated with that Lokuttara Marga is called 'Lokuttara Citta'.

The Lokuttara Marga arises four times in the mind of a single person, each time being more powerful than the previous one. The Lokuttara Marga that arises for the first time is named 'Sotapatti Magga'. The Lokuttara Marga that arises for the second time is named 'Sakadagami Magga'. The Lokuttara Marga that arises for the third time is named 'Anagami Magga'. The Lokuttara Marga that arises for the fourth time is named 'Arahatta Magga'. According to the Lokuttara Marga arising in four times -

  1. Sotapatti Magga Citta
  2. Sakadagami Magga Citta
  3. Anagami Magga Citta
  4. Arahatta Magga Citta

Thus, there are four Lokuttara Kusala Cittas.

For Lokuttara Vipakas, the name "Phala" (Fruit) is mostly used instead of the name "Vipaka". There is no difference in meaning between the two words Vipaka and Phala. Lokuttara Kusala is a Kusala that produces its result immediately, not delaying its result like Lokiya Kusalas.

  1. Sotapatti Phala Citta
  2. Sakadagami Phala Citta
  3. Anagami Phala Citta
  4. Arahatta Phala Citta

Thus, there are four Lokuttara Vipaka Cittas. There are no Lokuttara Kriya Cittas. With four Kusala Cittas and four Vipaka Cittas, there are eight Lokuttara Cittas. There are no Lokuttara Kriya Cittas because each Magga Citta does not arise more than once in a single person. Magga Cittas do not arise again and again because there is nothing more to eradicate in the defilements that have already been eradicated.

With fifty-four (54) Kamavacara Cittas, fifteen (15) Rupavacara Cittas, twelve (12) Arupavacara Cittas, and eight (8) Lokuttara Cittas, there are eighty-nine (89) Cittas in the brief method.

In the eighty-nine Cittas, there are twelve (12) Akusala, twenty-one (21) Kusala, thirty-six (36) Vipaka, and twenty (20) Kriya. These should be found.

These eighty-nine Cittas are divided into two parts as Ahetuka Cittas and Sahetuka Cittas, into two parts as Sobhana Cittas and Asobhana Cittas, and into two parts as Lokiya Cittas and Lokuttara Cittas. The numbers of Cittas belonging to each part should be found. The eight Lokuttara Cittas cannot be divided by distinctions such as feeling.
