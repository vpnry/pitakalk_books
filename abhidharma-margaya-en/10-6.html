6. Ten Iddhis

share

There are ten Iddhis: Adhitthaniddhi, Vikubbaniddhi, Manomayiddhi, Nanavipphariddhi, Samadhivipphariddhi, Ariyiddhi, Kammavipakajiddhi, Punniddhi, Vijjamayiddhi, Sammappayogapaccayiddhi.

One becoming a hundred, a thousand, becoming visible, becoming invisible, going through walls and mountains without sticking, diving into the ground as if in water etc. is called Adhitthaniddhi.

Abandoning one's natural form and appearing like a deva, or like a yaksha or rakshasa, or like an animal such as a lion or tiger is called Vikubbaniddhi.

Creating another complete body within one's body by mind and taking it out is called Manomayiddhi. Only the mentioned three Iddhis are accomplished by Abhinna power. The occurrence of certain things by the power of a certain high knowledge when it arises, or before it arises, or after it arises, is Nanavipphariddhi. If the Arahant Path Knowledge is definitely to arise in someone in that life, that person will not die due to any reason without obtaining it. In childhood, Venerable Bakkula was swallowed by a fish. But his life was saved. That is Nanavipphariddhi. The protection of Venerable Sankicca's life in childhood, and the protection of Venerable Bhutapala's life is also Nanavipphariddhi.

The occurrence of certain things by the power of Samadhi during the time of staying in Samadhi, or before or after it, is Samadhivipphariddhi. When Venerable Sariputta was staying at Kapotakandara, a yaksha hit him a blow hard enough to crush even an elephant. At that occasion, since he was in attainment, he did not feel any pain. That is Samadhivipphariddhi. Seeing Venerable Sanjiva, a disciple of Kakusandha Buddha, staying in Nirodha Samapatti at the foot of a tree, people thought "This monk has died while sitting", piled wood on him and set fire. The fire burned until the wood was finished. Not even his robes were harmed by it. That too is Samadhivipphariddhi.

Since the mind is mastered, Arahants can dwell without loathing towards things others loathe. They can also generate loathing towards things others consider high. They can also dwell with equanimity towards all things, loathsome and not loathsome. That Iddhi of Arahants is called Ariyiddhi.

The Iddhi possessed by birds, devas, some humans, and spirits is Kammavipakajiddhi. Those who lived with Iddhi were the humans of the first aeon.

The Iddhi of Universal Monarchs and the Iddhi possessed by some meritorious persons like Jotiya Jatila is Punniddhi.

The ability to perform various things by various sciences existing in the world is called Vijjamayiddhi.

Abandoning defilements by right exertion reckoned as practice and accomplishing various other things by means is called Sammappayogapaccayiddhi.
