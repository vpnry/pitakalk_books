Kilesa

share

There are ten Kilesas namely Lobha - Dosa - Moha - Mana - Ditthi - Vicikiccha - Thina - Uddhacca - Ahirika - Anottappa.

Lobhadi ten are called Kilesa in the sense of Dhammas that burn and scorch the continuity of beings, and in the sense of Dhammas that defile and bring to a low state. The nature of these Dhammas should be understood as shown in the Cetasika chapter. Out of these Dhammas, the burning and scorching nature is excessive in Lobha - Dosa. It should be said that the defiling nature exists in all without difference.

One Thousand Five Hundred Kilesas

The method of counting one thousand five hundred Kilesas is as follows. Since all Cittas are similar in the characteristic of cognizing the object, they are counted as one Dhamma. The group of Dhammas namely Citta and fifty-two Cetasikas is called fifty-three Nama Dhammas. When the seventy-five Dhammas namely fifty-three Nama Dhammas, eighteen Nipphanna Rupas, and four Laksana Rupas are doubled as internal and external, it becomes one hundred and fifty. Kilesas arise taking those one hundred and fifty Dhammas as objects. Therefore, those one hundred and fifty Dhammas are multiplied by the ten Kilesas. When done so, it becomes one thousand five hundred. That is called one thousand five hundred Kilesas.
