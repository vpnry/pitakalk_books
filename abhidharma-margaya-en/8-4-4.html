10th Lesson

Dukkha Arya Satya, Dukkha Samudaya Arya Satya, Dukkha Nirodha Arya Satya, Dukkha Nirodhagamini Patipada Arya Satya are the four Arya Satyas.

Truth (Satya) is what definitely exists, exists exactly in that way and not in any other way. These truths are named Arya Satya by adding the word Arya to the beginning not just by saying Satya, for several reasons. Some truths are true in one way but become untrue when looked at in another way. There is no becoming untrue in any way in the four truths such as Dukkha etc. preached by the Buddha. They are exactly so. The truth that does not change in any way is the noble truth among truths. Here the word Arya is added to the beginning of the word Satya to show the nobility of these truths. The noble truth is called Arya Satya.

These four truths were preached by the Buddha. The Buddha is called Arya because he is worshipped by the world including Devas. Since these are things seen and preached to the world by the Arya Buddha, these truths belong to the Buddha. In the sense that they are the truths of the Arya, they are called Arya Satyas.

If one realizes these four truths, that person transcends the state of Puthujjana and reaches the state of Arya Puggala. Since they cause the person who realizes them to reach the Arya state, these truths are also called Arya Satya.

Dukkha Arya Satya

The eighty-one Lokiya Cittas, the fifty-one Cetasikas other than Lobha, and the twenty-eight Rupas - this group of Dhammas belongs to Dukkha Satya.

The suffering of birth, the suffering of decay, the suffering of death, the suffering of having to sorrow and weep due to loss of relatives etc., the suffering of excessive mental fatigue due to loss of relatives etc., the suffering of association with the unpleasant, the suffering of separation from the pleasant, the suffering of not getting what one wants - all these arise and have as their object the group of Dhammas said to belong to Dukkha Satya. Among those Dhammas, there is not a single Dhamma that is free from these three: Jati, Jara, Marana. Therefore, that group of Dhammas is definitely suffering (Dukkha). Because they are definitely suffering, they are called Dukkha Satya.

Furthermore, because the person who is attached to that mass of Sankhara Dhammas called Dukkha Satya is afflicted by the three sufferings namely Sankhara Dukkha, Viparinama Dukkha, and Dukkha Dukkha, those Dhammas are definitely suffering.

Of those three sufferings, Sankhara Dukkha is the suffering that arises regarding producing something first and maintaining it. To be born as a human in the human world, there must be some merit. Without merit, a human Pancaskandha cannot be obtained. Therefore, the one who wishes to obtain it must do merit for it. Having to do merit is one suffering that arises regarding the human Pancaskandha. Even if merit is done, if many sins are committed, it is not obtained. Therefore, one must refrain from sins. Refraining from sins is also not an easy task. Having to refrain from sins is also one suffering regarding producing the human Pancaskandha. After being born in the human world, human life is not protected automatically. One has to strive hard to provide food and drink and to protect it without letting it perish. That striving is the suffering regarding maintaining life after birth. All that is the Sankhara Dukkha produced by the human Pancaskandha.

Viparinama Dukkha is the suffering caused by the decay and breaking up of the arisen Sankharas. However this human Pancaskandha is nourished, it changes by decaying. It can be destroyed by not receiving food etc. as needed or by not receiving requisites as needed or by some other danger or by coming to death. By the exhaustion of life span, it will definitely come to death someday and be completely destroyed. It cannot be prevented by any power. When one sees causes such as diseases by which Pancaskandha can be destroyed, one has to strive hard to protect it. When signs of destruction are seen, great fear and sorrow also arise. All that is Viparinama Dukkha regarding the human Pancaskandha.

The painful feelings arising in various places of the body due to various causes from time to time and mental painful feelings arising due to various causes are called Dukkha Dukkha. In one lifetime, many such sufferings arise. If there is no human Pancaskandha, not even one of those sufferings arises. Therefore, it must be said that all those sufferings are produced by the human Pancaskandha itself. Similarly, that threefold suffering is produced by other Pancaskandhas as well. All Sankharas said to belong to Dukkha Satya above produce the threefold suffering for the person who is attached to them. Therefore, that group of Sankharas is the true suffering or Dukkha Satya.

Dukkha Samudaya Arya Satya

The true cause of the arising of suffering is called Dukkha Samudaya Arya Satya. That is Tanha. The Tanha meant here is the nature of attachment to the Pancaskandha taking it as a good thing, as a place where happiness exists. As long as that Tanha exists without being abandoned, the being acts to maintain the obtained Pancaskandha and to obtain future Pancaskandhas. It is to those actions that Kamma is said. As long as the being does Kamma, he receives Pancaskandha again and again. As long as Pancaskandha is received, the being is not freed from the said three sufferings. Therefore, that Tanha is called the true cause of producing suffering.

Dukkha Nirodha Arya Satya

Nibbana is called Dukkha Nirodha Arya Satya. As long as Pancaskandha exists, the said three kinds of suffering also exist. The three kinds of suffering do not exist when Pancaskandha is extinguished. Therefore, the cessation of Pancaskandha is called Dukkha Nirodha Arya Satya.

Dukkha Nirodhagamini Patipada Arya Satya

The eight Maggangas namely Samma Ditthi, Samma Sankappa, Samma Vaca, Samma Kammanta, Samma Ajiva, Samma Vayama, Samma Sati, Samma Samadhi arising in the Lokuttara Magga Citta are that Dukkha Nirodhagamini Patipada Arya Satya. Since this suffering comes again and again as long as Tanha exists, the only way to escape from suffering is destroying the Tanha that produces suffering. The said Lokuttara Magga arises destroying that Tanha. The only thing that has the power to destroy Tanha is that Noble Eightfold Path. Therefore, it is called Dukkha Nirodhagamini Patipada Arya Satya. (It should also be noted that everywhere here Pancaskandha refers only to Lokiya Dhammas.)

Caturarya Satya in Brief

The eighty-one Lokiya Cittas, fifty-one Cetasikas, twenty-eight Rupas - these are Dukkha Satya. Tanha or Lobha Cetasika is Samudaya Satya. Nibbana is Nirodha Satya. The eight Maggangas arising in the four Lokuttara Kusala Cittas are Magga Satya.

The four Lokuttara Kusala Cittas, the Cetasikas other than the eight Maggangas arising in them, the four Phala Cittas and the Cetasikas arising in them - these Dhammas are not included in the Satya Sangaha. They do not belong to Caturarya Satya.

Thus ends the Seventh Chapter named Samuccaya Sangaha.
