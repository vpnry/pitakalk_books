Asava

share

There are four Asavas namely Kamasava - Bhavasava - Ditthasava - Avijjasava.

The gross Lobha which is the desire for the five sense objects namely Rupa - Sabda - Gandha - Rasa - Sprastavya is called Kamasava.

The desire existing for Rupavacara - Arupavacara Kusalas and for Rupa-Arupa Bhavas obtained as their Vipaka is called Bhavasava. Lobha Cetasika is taken by both Kamasava and Bhavasava.

All Ditthis are called Ditthasava.

Moha Cetasika which covers the true nature of Dhammas is called Avijjasava.

When taken by way of Dhammas, Asavas are the three Cetasikas namely Lobha - Ditthi - Moha.

Water mixed with substances and fermented for a long time is called Asava. That water has the nature of intoxicating those who drink it. The above mentioned Lobhadi Dhammas are called "Asava" because they are similar to Asava (liquor) due to being fermented for a long time and due to intoxicating. Those three Lobhadi Dhammas are similar to Asava because they have fermented in the continuity of beings for a long time in Samsara and because they intoxicate beings.

Also, those Dhammas are called "Asava" in the sense of oozing out, flowing. Those Lobhadi Dhammas ooze out from doors like Cakkhu etc. like pus oozing from a wound. Those Dhammas flow overwhelming mundane Dhammas up to Gotrabhu Citta and overwhelming the world up to Bhavagra.
