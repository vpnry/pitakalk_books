1. Importance of Abhidharma

share

The Dhamma that analyzes and shows the root elements that make up all beings great and small such as gods, humans, elephants, horses, cattle, flies, mosquitoes etc., and all objects such as soil, rocks, water, wind, trees, creepers etc., and teaches how those root elements arise and exist as a lineage, and analyzes and shows the element of Nibbana is Abhidharma.

It is only by learning Abhidharma that one can correctly know that these beings such as humans living now existed in the past, that they will be born again and again after death, and how rebirth occurs.

It is only by learning Abhidharma that one can correctly understand that there is something called merit, there is something called demerit, the power in those merits and demerits, and how merits and demerits give happiness and suffering to beings.

It is only by learning Abhidharma that one can correctly know that these are merits and these are demerits.

It is only by learning Abhidharma that one can know that there are many beings belonging to various classes such as Devas and Brahmas with various forms and various kinds of happiness and suffering, invisible to our eyes, both near us and far away from us.

It is only by learning Abhidharma that one can correctly understand what Nibbana is and what the path to Nibbana is.

One does not accept that there is a future birth, does not accept that beings receive happiness and suffering according to kamma, does not accept that there are hells and that there are gods and brahmas, because of not knowing this Dhamma.

Wrong views such as "there is no need to worship Buddha statues", "there is no need to offer flowers, lamps, food etc. to the inanimate Buddha" arise because of not knowing this Dhamma.

Even if one is a Buddhist, a person who does not know this Dhamma even to some extent is a blind worldling (Andha Puthujjana). He cannot understand well by listening to a sermon or reading a Dhamma book. To be able to understand well a sermon that is delivered, and to be able to understand well by reading a Dhamma book, one must have learnt Abhidharma at least to some extent.

The Dhamma of the Buddha is divided into three parts: Sutta Pitaka, Vinaya Pitaka, and Abhidharma Pitaka. The Abhidharma Pitaka consists of seven books (Prakaranas).

  1. Dhammasangani Prakarana
  2. Vibhanga Prakarana
  3. Dhatukatha Prakarana
  4. Puggalapannatti Prakarana
  5. Kathavatthu Prakarana
  6. Yamaka Prakarana
  7. Patthana Prakarana

are the names of those seven books.
