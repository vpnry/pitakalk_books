3. Five Sense Fields

share

Rupa - Sabda - Gandha - Rasa - Sprastavya are the five Gocara Rupas. These five Rupas are the objects for the five Prasadas such as Cakkhu etc. Therefore, they are called Gocara Rupas. Since they are grasped by the five Prasadas such as Cakkhu etc., they are also called Visaya Rupas.

Rupa

Here, what is called Rupa is the color that strikes the Cakkhu Prasada and is grasped by it. It is also called Varna Rupa. Only this Varna Rupa strikes the Cakkhu Prasada and is grasped by it. Except for color, objects that are grasped by the hand are not seen by the eye. Where it is said "Rupakkhandha", the word Rupa refers to the twenty-eight Rupas. This Varna Rupa is also one Rupa belonging to those twenty-eight. In the context of Gocara Rupas, the word Rupa refers only to the Varna Dhatu. The name Rupa is given to all Rupas in the sense of 'that which undergoes change due to cold, heat etc.'. The name Rupa is given to Varna Dhatu in the sense of 'that which is seen'. Of the twenty-eight Rupas, only this Varna Rupa is seen by the eye. Other Rupas are not seen by the eye.

Sabda

The thing that strikes the Sota Prasada and is known by means of Sota Prasada is Sabda (Sound). It is a Rupa that arises from striking and bursting and vanishes quickly. Saying 'vanishes quickly' does not mean that its lifespan is shorter than other Rupas, but that its continuity does not last long.

Gandha

The Rupa that strikes the Ghana Prasada and is known by means of Ghana Prasada is Gandha (Smell). It exists excessively in some objects and slightly in some objects. Although Gandha exists in some measure in every object where the four elements exist, the Gandha in some objects is not felt because it is very subtle.

Rasa

The Rupa that strikes the Jivha Prasada and is known by means of the tongue is Rasa (Taste). It becomes manifold according to the differences of the Great Essential Elements. Although Rasa exists in every object, Rasa is not felt in some objects because they do not dissolve and because it is very subtle. To be able to know the taste, it must dissolve and touch the Jivha Prasada.

Sprastavya

The Rupa that strikes the Kaya Prasada and is known by means of Kaya Prasada is called Sprastavya (Tangible). It is not a separate Rupa like Rupa, Sabda etc. What strikes the Kaya Prasada are the three Great Essentials: Pathavi, Tejo, and Vayo. What is felt to the body as hard nature is Pathavi Dhatu. The cold nature and hot nature felt to the body is Tejo Dhatu. The moving, pushing nature felt to the body is Vayo Dhatu. Apo Dhatu is not something that is touched. Since Rupas that are objects for all five Prasadas must be shown when showing Gocara Rupas, the three Bhuta Rupas which are objects for Kaya Prasada are shown by the name Sprastavya. When counting Rupas, a Rupa called Sprastavya Rupa is not counted.

In one object, all of Rupa - Gandha - Rasa - Sprastavya exist. When an object is held by the hand, what is grasped by the hand and felt is the Pathavi Dhatu in it. What is seen by the eye is the Varna Rupa. The part seen by the eye is not grasped by the hand. The part grasped by the hand is not seen by the eye. The parts felt to the nose and tongue in it are felt only to the nose and tongue, not to the hand. Not seen by the eye. Thinking that what is grasped by the hand and what is seen by the eye are the same thing is a wrong understanding due to not knowing the analysis of Dhamma.

Two Bhava Rupas

There are two Bhava Rupas called Stri Bhava Rupa and Purusa Bhava Rupa. The Rupa that imparts the female nature to the body of a being is Stri Bhava Rupa. The Rupa that imparts the male nature is Purusa Bhava Rupa. These two Rupas are two Rupas that exercise their power over the bodily Rupas. Therefore, Stri Bhava Rupa is also called "Itthindriya" and Purusa Bhava Rupa is also called "Purusindriya". Bhava Rupas arise for beings simultaneously with the Patisandhi Citta. Everyone's body is created from the Four Great Essentials. There is no male-female difference in them. When the body is formed from the Great Essentials, the body with Stri Bhava Rupa is formed in the female form by its influence. The body with Purusa Bhava Rupa is formed in the male form by its influence. Bhava Rupas are spread everywhere in the body.

Hadaya Vatthu Rupa

The places for the arising of Cakkhu Vinnana etc. are the five Prasada Rupas. The Rupa which is the place for the arising of the remaining Cittas except the five Vinnanas is the Hadaya Vatthu Rupa. It exists in the blood inside the heart cavity.

Jivitindriya Rupa

The Rupa that protects and causes to live the Rupas arising bound with itself so that they last for a long time in continuity is called Jivitindriya. In a body, there are Rupas produced by four causes: Kamma - Citta - Utu - Ahara. Of those four causes, the three causes Citta - Utu - Ahara exist near the Rupas produced by them. Therefore, a separate thing is not needed to govern the Rupas produced by Citta etc. causes. Kammaja Rupas in the continuity of a being are produced by a Kamma that is not present at that occasion, but has arisen and ceased in the past. Therefore, there must be another thing that protects the Kammaja Rupa continuities to enable them to last for a long time. The power that protects Kammaja Rupas and maintains their continuity for a long time is this Jivitindriya Rupa. By the influence of Jivitindriya, Kammaja Rupa continuities exist unbroken for hundreds and thousands of years.

Ahara Rupa

The nutritive essence in food which produces a separate group of Rupas in the body, strengthens it and maintains it for a long time is Ahara Rupa. That nutritive essence generally exists in all objects. However, the nutritive essence in all things is not suitable for all beings. Respective beings eat food suitable for their respective bodies. Their bodies are sustained by the nutritive essence in them.

These eighteen Rupas mentioned from Pathavi Dhatu up to Ahara Rupa are Rupas produced directly by causes such as Kamma. Therefore, they are called Nipphanna Rupas. Furthermore, they are also called Svabhava Rupa - Svalaksana Rupa - Rupa Rupa.
