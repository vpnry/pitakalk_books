Eighteen Dhatus

share

Cakkhu Dhatu, Sota Dhatu, Ghana Dhatu, Jivha Dhatu, Kaya Dhatu, Rupa Dhatu, Saddha Dhatu, Gandha Dhatu, Rasa Dhatu, Photthabba Dhatu, Cakkhu Vinnana Dhatu, Sota Vinnana Dhatu, Ghana Vinnana Dhatu, Jivha Vinnana Dhatu, Kaya Vinnana Dhatu, Mano Dhatu, Mano Vinnana Dhatu, Dhamma Dhatu are the eighteen Dhatus.

The meaning of the word Dhatu is that which is not a being, not a person. Cakkhu Prasada itself is called Cakkhu Dhatu because it is a nature that is not a being or a person. Sota Dhatu etc. are also the respective Prasada Rupas. Vanna Rupa itself is called Rupa Dhatu because it is not a being or a person. Saddha - Gandha - Rasa - Photthabba Dhatus are respectively Sabda - Gandha - Rasa - Sprastavya. The single Manayatana is divided into seven Dhatus here.

Of the eighty-nine Cittas, the two Cakkhu Vinnanas are Cakkhu Vinnana Dhatu. The two Sota Vinnanas are Sota Vinnana Dhatu. The two Ghana Vinnanas are Ghana Vinnana Dhatu. The two Jivha Vinnanas are Jivha Vinnana Dhatu. The two Kaya Vinnanas are Kaya Vinnana Dhatu. The three Cittas namely Pancadvaravajjana and the two Sampaticchanas are Mano Dhatu. The remaining seventy-six Cittas are Mano Vinnana Dhatu. The fifty-two Cetasikas, sixteen Sukshma Rupas, and Nibbana are Dhamma Dhatu.

What is divided into eighteen as Dhatus is the same group of Dhammas that was divided into twelve as Ayatanas. The single Manayatana is divided into seven in the Dhatu classification as Cakkhu Vinnana Dhatu, Sota Vinnana Dhatu, Ghana Vinnana Dhatu, Jivha Vinnana Dhatu, Kaya Vinnana Dhatu, Mano Dhatu, Mano Vinnana Dhatu. That is the difference between the two parts Ayatana and Dhatu. Some have strong soul perception regarding Vinnana. Their soul perception is not dispelled by doing Vipassana by way of Ayatana method which has no analysis of Vinnana. Therefore, this Dhatu classification teaching which analyzes Vinnana further has been done for those who have strong soul perception in Vinnana to make it easy for them to dispel their soul perception.
