Ten Imperfections of Vipassana

share

Obhasa (Illumination), Piti (Joy), Passaddhi (Tranquillity), Adhimokkha (Resolution), Paggaha (Exertion), Sukha (Happiness), Nana (Knowledge), Upatthana (Mindfulness), Upekkha (Equanimity), Nikanti (Attachment) - these ten are the imperfections of Vipassana. Imperfections of Vipassana are a group of factors that mislead the yogi and obstruct Vipassana. They do not arise for yogis who have not done Vipassana well. Their arising is a sign that Vipassana has been done well up to that point.

Obhasa is a special light visible only to the yogi and not to others, arising solely from the power of Vipassana. For some yogis, it arises such that only their body is visible, for some such that the entire room they are in is visible, and for some such that a distance of leagues - yojanas is visible.

Piti is the strong joy that arises due to the goodness of Vipassana knowledge and seeing the arising and passing away of formations very well.

Passaddhi is the tranquillity of body and mind arising from Vipassana. Due to that tranquillity, the yogi can engage in meditation as long as he wishes without any difficulty. He does not even feel the passage of time.

Adhimokkha is strong faith. At this stage, a strong faith that pleases the mind exceedingly arises in the yogi along with the Vipassana consciousness.

Paggaha is effort. At this stage, a good effort which is neither too slack nor too rapid arises in the yogi along with the Vipassana consciousness.

Sukha is a strong happiness that pleases the entire body, spreading throughout the entire body, arising with the Vipassana consciousness.

Nana is the very sharp Vipassana knowledge that penetrates into material and immaterial dhammas.

Upatthana is the mindfulness mental factor associated with Vipassana consciousness. At this stage, a strong mindfulness arises in the yogi that is established on the formations being contemplated, without the mind shaking like a mountain.

Upekkha is the two equanimities: Tatramajjhattata Upekkha and Avajjana Upekkha.

Nikanti is the subtle craving that attaches to the Vipassana adorned with illumination etc. It is a very subtle greed that is difficult to recognize as a defilement. The real imperfection of Vipassana is that Nikanti. Illumination etc. become imperfections because they are causes for Nikanti.

When illumination etc. appear, the unskilled yogi thinks "Never before has such a light - such joy - such wisdom arisen in me. Now these wonderful things are arising in me because I have attained the Path and Fruit," and abandons meditation. He behaves saying he has become a Sotapanna without being a Sotapanna, or saying he has become an Arahant without being an Arahant. The skilled yogi understands that a subtle craving arises regarding illumination etc., and if it grows, he will fall away from the path to Nirvana and fall back into the path of Samsara. To remove that craving, he contemplates illumination etc. as impermanent, as suffering because they cause fear, and as non-self because they are unsubstantial, and removes the craving arising regarding illumination etc. The knowledge that understands the characteristics of the true path and the false path of the yogi who has thus considered the obstructive dhammas to Vipassana such as illumination as impermanent etc. and removed the craving, is the Maggamagga Nanadassana Visuddhi.
