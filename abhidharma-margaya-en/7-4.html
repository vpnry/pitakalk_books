4. Ten Non-concrete Matter

share

Akasa Dhatu

Akasa Dhatu is spoken of in two ways: Ajatakasa and Rupa Paricchedakasa. The space of the world which is the place for the existence of all objects and persons is called Ajatakasa. The space that separates Rupa Kalapas in solid objects from one another is called Pariccheda Akasa. What is counted as one of the twenty-eight Rupas is that Pariccheda Akasa. This Akasa Dhatu is not a real Paramattha Dhamma with a separate arising. However, since Akasa is also needed between Rupa Kalapas for their unmixed existence, it is counted as one Rupa.

Vikara Rupas

Kaya Vinnatti

The special force that turns the speed of the Cittaja Vayo which moves bodily limbs in various directions suitable for respective actions is called Kaya Vinnatti. When a Citta associated with the effort to perform a certain action arises, by the power of that mind, just as light spreads throughout a dark room when a match is struck, air (Vayo) arises throughout the bodily limbs that need to be moved. Bodily limbs move by that speed of air. The Vinnatti Rupa also arises together with that air. By it, the speed of air is directed to the required direction. Upward and downward movements etc. of bodily limbs happen as required for the action due to that direction.

Vaci Vinnatti

The special force that directs the sound so that required letters and required words are formed when making an effort to speak is called Vaci Vinnatti. Sound arises by the striking of Cittaja Vayo at respective places. That sound arises as required for the matter due to this Vaci Vinnatti Rupa. The name Vinnatti is given to these because the intention of the person is intimated by them.

Lahutadi Three Rupas

The lightness of bodily Rupas is Lahuta. Softness - absence of hardness is Muduta. Suitability for action or wieldiness is Kammannata. These three Rupas are three qualities of other Rupas. Due to bad weather, bad mind, taking unsuitable food, not getting enough food, occurrence of various diseases etc., a heavy nature in the body unable to work quickly, a hard nature causing pain in bending and stretching, and a nature unable to move the body as required arise. When the weather is good, mind is good, suitable food and drink are obtained etc., these Rupas called Lahuta, Muduta, Kammannata arise in the body.

These five Rupas namely two Vinnatti Rupas and three Lahutadi Rupas are called Vikara Rupas. They are special modes that arise in the Rupa body from time to time. The name Vikara Rupa is given for that reason.

Four Laksana Rupas

Upacaya - Santati - Jarata - Aniccata are the four Laksana Rupas. Both words Upacaya and Santati refer to the arising of Rupas. The arising of Rupas before the Rupas that should arise for a being are completed is called Upacaya. The arising of Rupas that appear again and again replacing the Rupas that break up after the Rupas are completed is called Santati. The decaying of Rupas is Jarata. The breaking up is Aniccata. Since these are characteristics of other Rupas, they are called Laksana Rupas.

These ten Rupas namely Pariccheda Rupa, five Vikara Rupas, and four Laksana Rupas are called Anipphanna Rupas because they are not Rupas produced directly by causes such as Kamma.

Among these Rupas, the lifespan of the two Vinnatti Rupas is one Citta Khana. The lifespan of Upacaya and Santati Rupas is one small Khana (sub-moment). The lifespan of Jarata Rupa is forty-nine small Khanas. The lifespan of Aniccata Rupa is one small Khana. The lifespan of the remaining twenty-two Rupas is seventeen Citta Khanas.
