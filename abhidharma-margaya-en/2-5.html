5. The Fifty-Four Kamavacara Cittas

share

There are fifty-four Kamavacara Cittas: twelve Akusala Cittas, eighteen Ahetuka Cittas, and twenty-four Kamavacara Sobhana Cittas.

Akusala Cittas (Unwholesome Consciousness)

Cittas which, if they arise in a person's mind, produce unpleasant results for him in the future are called Akusala Cittas.

There are three Akusala Roots: Lobha (Greed), Dosa (Hatred), and Moha (Delusion). These three are three Cetasikas. The Akusala Citta, which is generally one, is divided into three based on the association with qualities like Lobha: Lobhamula Citta, Dosamula Citta, and Mohamula Citta.

The Eight Lobhamula Cittas

Lobha is the nature of attachment to various things and persons, thinking "this is good, beautiful, sweet". Desire, want, lust, greed, attachment, affection, love are words that refer to Lobha itself. The Citta associated with that Lobha is further divided into two: Somanassa-sahagata Citta (Citta accompanied by pleasure) and Upekkha-sahagata Citta (Citta accompanied by equanimity).

It has been stated above that Citta is the knowing of something. In this Dhamma, the thing that is felt by the Citta is called Arammana (Object). Since one Citta is known by another Citta, Citta is also an object. Every object has one of three qualities: goodness, badness, or neutrality. These qualities are called the 'taste' of the object. If a Citta arises taking any object, a nature that enjoys or experiences the taste of that object arises bound with that Citta. The nature arising with the Citta that experiences the sweetness of the object is called Somanassa Vedana (Pleasant Feeling). The nature that experiences the neutrality of the object is called Upekkha Vedana (Neutral Feeling). The nature that experiences the badness of the object is called Domanassa Vedana (Unpleasant Feeling). Lobha-sahagata Citta is never associated with Domanassa Vedana. When a Lobhamula Citta arises, it arises with the experiencing of either the goodness or the neutrality of the object.

The Lobhamula Citta is divided into two as Somanassa-sahagata Citta and Upekkha-sahagata Citta based on the two factors: experiencing the goodness of the object and experiencing the neutrality of the object. Somanassa-sahagata Citta is the Citta associated with Somanassa Vedana. Upekkha-sahagata Citta is the Citta associated with Upekkha Vedana. Happiness is another name used for Somanassa Vedana.

Each of these two Cittas is again divided into two. That is as follows: Sometimes this Lobhamula Somanassa-sahagata Citta is associated with the wrong view that "there is no such thing as merit and sin, there is no result of merit and sin, there is no other world, there is no fault in doing anything". Sometimes it is not associated with wrong view. View is also called Ditthi. Based on the two factors of being associated with view or not, the Somanassa-sahagata Citta from the two Lobhamula Cittas mentioned above is divided into two as Somanassa-sahagata Ditthigata-sampayutta Citta and Somanassa-sahagata Ditthigata-vippayutta Citta. Similarly, the Upekkha-sahagata Citta is divided into two as Upekkha-sahagata Ditthigata-sampayutta Citta and Upekkha-sahagata Ditthigata-vippayutta Citta. By this division, there are four Lobhamula Cittas.

The first of those four Cittas, the Somanassa-sahagata Ditthigata-sampayutta Citta, is again divided into two. That is as follows: Sometimes that Citta arises naturally. Sometimes it arises due to the inducement of others or by one's own effort or by the effort of others. The Citta that arises without inducement or effort is called Asankharika Citta. The Citta that arises with inducement or effort is called Sasankharika Citta. The remaining three Cittas are also divided into two each based on the Sankhara distinction. When divided thus, there are eight Lobhamula Cittas. Those eight Cittas are as follows:

  1. Somanassa-sahagata Ditthigata-sampayutta Asankharika Citta
  2. Somanassa-sahagata Ditthigata-sampayutta Sasankharika Citta
  3. Somanassa-sahagata Ditthigata-vippayutta Asankharika Citta
  4. Somanassa-sahagata Ditthigata-vippayutta Sasankharika Citta
  5. Upekkha-sahagata Ditthigata-sampayutta Asankharika Citta
  6. Upekkha-sahagata Ditthigata-sampayutta Sasankharika Citta
  7. Upekkha-sahagata Ditthigata-vippayutta Asankharika Citta
  8. Upekkha-sahagata Ditthigata-vippayutta Sasankharika Citta
