5. Summary of Objects

share

Part 1

“Arammana” (Object) is called for things that are felt - caught by the mind - grasped by the mind.

There are six objects according to doors: Rupammana, Saddarammana, Gandharammana, Rasarammana, Photthabbarammana, Dhammarammana.

The color in matters is Rupammana. Only Rupammana is seen by the eye. It is the color that is seen by the eye, not the objects. Everything has a color. But the color of very subtle things is not seen by our eyes.

The thing felt by the Sothindriya or ear is called Saddarammana.

Good and bad smells felt by the nose are called Gandharammana.

Tastes felt by the tongue are called Rasarammana.

Hardness - softness - coldness - heat etc. felt by the Kayindriya are called Photthabbarammana.

All things felt by the mind - caught by the mind excluding the five objects form etc. are called Dhammarammana. Dhammarammana is sixfold as Pasada Rupa - Sukhuma Rupa - Citta - Cetasika - Nirvana - Pannatti. They will be known in the future. For now, just remember the six parts by name.

There are four parts of objects as Past - Future - Present - Time-freed. The thing that arose and ceased is called “Atitarammana”. The thing that will arise in the future is called “Anagatarammana”. The existing thing is called “Vattamanarammana”. This division of time is for things that have arising and ceasing. Nirvana and Pannatti do not have arising and ceasing. Therefore, they are not included in any of the three times. They are called “Kala Vimutta” (Time-freed). (This is a deep fact. Ask teachers and learn more.)

Only the present Rupammana is the object for the forty-six Cakkhudvarika Cittas. Past and future forms are not caught by Cakkhudvarika Cittas. Similarly, present sounds - smells - tastes - touches are objects for Cittas belonging to Sota - Ghana - Jivha - Kaya doors.

For Manodvarika Cittas, the six objects Form - Sound - Smell - Taste - Touch - Dhamma belonging to all three times and Nirvana - Pannatti which are Time-freed are objects.

For Dvaravimutta Cittas, the six objects which are present, past, or Pannatti called Kamma - Kamma Nimitta - Gati Nimitta that were objects for the death-proximate Javanas in the immediately preceding past existence are objects.

Part II

The objects of Cittas were shown above indefinitely according to doors. The object belonging to each Citta definitely should be known as stated below.

The two Cakkhuvinnanas take only Form as object. Sota - Ghana - Jivha - Kaya Vinnanas take Sound - Smell - Taste - Touch as objects.

The three Cittas Pancadvaravajjana - Sampaticchana called Manodhatu take the five objects Form etc. as objects.

The twelve Cittas: eleven Kamavacara Vipaka Cittas excluding Dvi Panca Vinnana and Manodhatu, and Hasituppada take Kamavacara Dhammas as objects. “Kamavacara Dhamma” is called for the fifty-four Kama Cittas, the Cetasikas associated with them, and the twenty-eight Rupas.

The twelve Akusala Cittas and the eight Nana Vippayutta Kamavacara Javana Cittas take all objects except the nine Lokuttara Dhammas. “Nine Lokuttara Dhammas” is called for the eight Lokuttara Cittas and Nirvana.

The five Cittas: four Nana Sampayutta Kamavacara Kusala Cittas and the Rupavacara Fifth Jhana Kusala Citta which has reached Abhinna state take all objects except Arahat Magga and Arahat Phala.

The six Cittas: four Nana Sampayutta Maha Kriya Cittas, Rupavacara Fifth Jhana Kriya Citta which has reached Abhinna state, and Manodvaravajjana take all objects.

“Sarvajnatajnana” (Omniscience) is called for the wisdom in the Maha Kriya Nana Sampayutta Cittas arising in Lord Buddhas. Just by attaining Jhana, one cannot perform Iddhi Pratiharya (Psychic Miracles). The power of Iddhi Pratiharya is only in the two Rupavacara Kusala Kriya Fifth Jhana Cittas. Even the ordinary Fifth Jhana Citta does not have that power. To get that power, the Fifth Jhana Citta must be further developed. The Fifth Jhana Citta thus developed is called “Abhinna Citta”. Whoever performs Iddhi Pratiharya, everyone does it with Abhinna Citta. Lokuttara Cittas do not have the ability to perform Iddhi Pratiharya.

Among Arupavacara Cittas, Akasanancayatana Citta is taken as object by the three Vinnanancayatana Cittas, and Akincannayatana Citta is taken as object by the three Nevasanna Nasannayatana Cittas. Since Akasanancayatana Vinnanancayatana Cittas are Mahaggata, the six Vinnanancayatana Nevasanna Nasannayatana Cittas taking them as objects are called “Mahaggatarammanika Cittas”.

The twenty-one Cittas: fifteen Rupavacara Cittas, three Akasanancayatana Cittas, and three Akincannayatana Cittas take Pannatti such as Kasina as objects.

Nirvana is taken as object by Lokuttara Cittas.

There are twenty-five Cittas taking Kamavacara Dhammas as objects. There are six Cittas taking Mahaggata Dhammas as objects. There are twenty-one Cittas taking Pannatti as objects. There are eight Cittas taking Nirvana as objects. There are twenty Cittas taking all objects except Lokuttara Dhammas. There are five Cittas taking all objects except Arahat Magga and Phala. There are six Cittas taking all objects.
