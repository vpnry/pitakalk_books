Citta Visuddhi

share

The strong concentration that keeps the mind on the meditation object without allowing hindrances such as sensual desire to enter and defile the mind is Citta Visuddhi. There are two types of yogis: Samatha Yanika and Vipassana Yanika. Yogis who first practice a Samatha meditation, and using the concentration obtained from it as a basis, practice Vipassana and obtain paths and fruits are Samatha Yanika. Yogis who do not engage in Samatha meditation but practice only Vipassana are Vipassana Yanika. Upacara Samadhi and Appana Samadhi obtained from Samatha meditation are the Citta Visuddhi of Samatha Yanikas. For those who practice only Vipassana, a certain concentration is obtained from it itself. Due to that concentration, the yogi's mind does not go astray and is well established on the meditation object. That concentration obtained from Vipassana itself is the Citta Visuddhi of Vipassana Yanikas.

Hindrances such as sensual desire enter the mind of the person who meditates without concentration from time to time and defile it. When there is strong concentration, the stream of meditation thoughts of the meditating yogi arises again and again on the meditation object in a unified manner. Thereby the mind remains pure. Since it keeps the yogi's mind pure in that way, concentration is called Citta Visuddhi.
