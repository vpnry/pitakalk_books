10. Occasions when Manodvara Vithis Arise

share

To have a good understanding of Manodvara Vithis, the occasions when they arise should be known. It was mentioned above that Saptarasayushka Vibhutarammana Avibhutarammana Manodvara Vithis arise taking as object the twenty-two present Rupas having a lifespan of seventeen Citta Khanas. The five objects Rupa, Sadda, Gandha, Rasa, Photthabba are included in those twenty-two Rupas.

If you go to Ruwanweli Mahaseya, you see that great stupa. Seeing Ruwanweli Seya means nothing else but the arising of some Cakkhudvara Vithi in your continuity (Santana) taking Ruwanweli Seya as object.

When you go home, you remember the Ruwanweli Mahaseya you saw. That remembering of Ruwanweli Mahaseya is the arising of a Manodvara Vithi taking as object the Ruwanweli Mahaseya you saw earlier.

The Ruwanweli Seya you saw is a group of Rupas having a lifespan of seventeen Citta Khanas. That lifespan of seventeen Citta Khanas passed and that group of Rupas ceased immediately. Now they are not there. The object for the Manodvara Vithi arising in your continuity as remembering the seen Ruwanweli Seya is a past group of Rupas which you saw at that time and which are not there now. What arose in you at that time is one of the two Manodvara Vithis not having seventeen Citta Khanas taking past Rupas as object.

If you turn towards the Ruwanweli Mahaseya which is not visible to the eye from home and worship it, what is subjected to that worship is not the Ruwanweli Mahaseya you saw earlier, but the Ruwanweli Seya existing there at that time. As worshipping it, what arises in you is some Vithi from the five Saptarasayushka Vibhutarammana Vithis and two Avibhutarammana Vithis taking the present Rupa as object. It is said that what is subjected to that worship is not the Ruwanweli Seya seen earlier, because all Rupas seen earlier by the eye as Ruwanweli Seya have ceased.

In worshipping Ruwanweli Mahaseya from home, the group of Rupa Kalapas which you consider as Ruwanweli Seya, having passed one Citta Khana after arising, can also be directed to your Manodvara. The Rupa Kalapas of Ruwanweli Mahaseya having passed two, three, four, five Citta Khanas can also come to your Manodvara. From them, the first, second, third, fourth, fifth Vibhutarammana Vithis arise in you. If Rupas having passed six or seven Citta Khanas are directed to the Manodvara, then Avibhutarammana Vithis arise.

If the Pannatti called Ruwanweli Mahaseya is directed to the Manodvara, then the Manodvara Vithi taking the Pannatti not having seventeen Citta Khanas as object arises.

If you remember that you saw Ruwanweli Seya, then what arose in you is a Manodvara Vithi taking Cakkhuvinnana or Cakkhudvara Vithi as object. That is a Manodvara Vithi arising taking Cittas as object. Remembering Ruwanweli Seya is one thing. Remembering that Ruwanweli Seya was seen is another thing. Understand the difference between those two without getting confused.

By seeing Ruwanweli Seya, sometimes great Piti (joy) might have arisen in you. Piti is a Cetasika. If you remember the Piti arisen by seeing Ruwanweli Seya, then a Manodvara Vithi taking Piti Cetasika as object arises.

Vedana is also a Cetasika. If you remember a Vedana arisen in you, then a Manodvara Vithi taking Vedana Cetasika as object arises. If you feel the Vedana of another person suffering from pain when you see him, then what arose is also a Manodvara Vithi taking Vedana Cetasika as object.

When remembering Adara (love), Kopa (anger), Mana (pride), Irshya (jealousy), Maitri (loving-kindness), Karuna (compassion) etc. arising in your continuity, and when feeling those things arising in others, Manodvara Vithi taking Cetasikas as object arises.

Only a few facts that can be easily understood were mentioned here. Thinking according to the mentioned facts, understand how Manodvara Vithis arise taking Rupa and Citta - Cetasikas as object.
